I am a PhD student at Stanford, very fortunate to be advised by Amin Saberi and Azalia Mirhoseini.

My recent research has focused on building more intelligent and efficient ML systems through multi-agent designs, scaling test-time computation, and bringing these capabilities to real-world applications. I have worked on Text-to-SQL systems, authoring CHESS, CHASE-SQL, and Reasoning-SQL with high efficiency and state-of-the-art accuracy on the BIRD leaderboard.

I received my Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from Sharif University of Technology. During my undergraduate studies, I had the opportunity to intern at IST Austria working on distributed optimization and federated learning under the supervision of Dan Alistarh, and at EPFL Switzerland on theoretical machine learning under the supervision of Emmanuel Abbe.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading books, playing table tennis, and sometimes solving and designing Mathematical Olympiad problems.
